Antipsychotics

  • Post by Alexia Walgren
  • June 1, 2020

Antipsychotics are a class of medication that can help with symptoms of psychosis. Most of these medications primarily help with positive symptoms of psychosis (definition here). There are two main types of antipsychotics; First generation antipsychotics (first used in the 50s) have potential to cause more motion-related side effects, but may still be the most effective medication for some patients. Second generation antipsychotics generally have less motion-related side effects, but still have side effects of their own. The medication that you or a loved one ends up taking will be largely decided through the symptoms that they experience and which side effects are least invasive in their life.

Too Long; Didn't Read: Schizophrenia is a disorder that involves psychosis for 6+ months. It can affect the young and the old. Treatment varies, and there is no cure. Support should be specialized to the individual.
