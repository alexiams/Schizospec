What is Schizophrenia?

  • Post by Alexia Walgren
  • June 2, 2018

"Schizophrenia is a chronic and severe mental disorder that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves"(NIMH, 2020).

Schizophrenia is defined by positive, negative, and cognitive symptoms (definitions here). Schizophrenia can only be diagnosed after 6 months of symptoms. Prior to that, it may be diagnosed as Schizophreniform. It can be incredibly hard to deal with shifting perceptions of yourself and the world around you. Often, the behavior exhibited by those with schizophrenia is seen as unacceptable to their peers.

People can be genetically predisposed for schizophrenia and similar disorders. However, a family history of the disorder does not necessarily mean that someone will inherit schizophrenia. It is important that family history is shared with healthcare providers when asked, as it may alter the way treatment plans are made.

Treatment of schizophrenia is based around stopping or minimizing symptoms. There is no cure for schizophrenia. Treatments include antipsychotics, which are a type of medication (More info here). There's also psychosocial treatment, which allows people to process their difficulties and develop more effective coping skills. This one is also known as "talk therapy". Coordinated specialty care (CSC) allows for integration of both of these along with family involvement (NIMH).

You must remember that schizophrenia affects individuals differently. Aside from the disorder showing different symptoms depending on the person, a young adult will not necessarily react the same as someone in their 40s. It's important that young people are given care and understanding in an environment that is overwhelmingly medical information and support for families of those effected.

Too Long; Didn't Read: Schizophrenia is a disorder that involves psychosis for 6+ months. It can affect the young and the old. Treatment varies, and there is no cure. Support should be specialized to the individual.
